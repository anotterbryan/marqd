The Dataset

What the archive actually knows.

Marq'd collects only what's needed to build a meaningful picture of queer geography over time. Nothing is traceable to an individual. Everything tells a story at scale.

◎

Geography

Every story is tied to real coordinates — a neighborhood, a city, a country. Together they build a living map of where queer life is happening and where it's being suppressed.

lat / lng / location_display
◷

Time

Submission timestamps and the year a story occurred create two separate timelines: when people are telling stories, and when those stories happened. The gap between them is its own data.

submitted_at / year_occurred
◈

Safety Index

When a contributor withholds their location, that's a signal. Aggregate withheld rates by region create a proxy safety index — showing where people most fear being found.

location_withheld
◉

Community Tags

Contributors tag their own stories — joy, fear, first time, community, loss, pride. These community-generated taxonomies reveal what queer life actually looks like from the inside.

tags[]
◐

Emotional Resonance

Fire and heart reactions show which stories move people most. Aggregated by region and tag, this data reveals what kinds of stories build the most connection across communities.

reactions_fire / reactions_heart
◑

Voice & Audio

Whether a story is told in someone's own voice, AI-narrated, or text-only tells us something about access, safety, and comfort. Audio data is never transcribed or analyzed.

audio_type / voice_id

The Silence Map

Blank spaces on the globe tell a story too.

When a region has zero submissions, that absence is not neutral. It reflects the reality of 64 countries where same-sex relationships are still criminalized — where a person cannot safely say where they are, let alone what they have lived.

Marq'd makes this visible. Silence on our globe is evidence. It's the kind of geographic testimony that advocates, researchers, and policymakers have never had access to before — not from survey data, not from official records.

This is why the archive exists.

// 64 countries currently criminalize same-sex relationships. 2 impose the death penalty: Iran and Afghanistan.
Partners & Research

Built for the community. Available to the people who fight for it.

Marq'd data is not for sale. It is available — anonymized and aggregated — to organizations and researchers working to document, protect, and advance queer life globally.

Academic Researchers

Studying queer geography, digital humanities, social movements, or LGBTQ+ health outcomes? Marq'd can provide anonymized aggregate datasets, geographic summaries, and tag distributions for academic research.

Data partnership

Advocacy Organizations

Organizations like ILGA World, Human Rights Watch, and OutRight Action International use geographic data to document persecution and push for policy change. Marq'd ground-level data can supplement their work.

Advocacy partner

Journalists & Documentarians

Marq'd data can provide geographic and temporal context for stories about queer life, safety, and community. We can provide regional summaries and trend data to support reporting.

Media partnership

Community Organizations

Local LGBTQ+ centers, health organizations, and crisis services can use regional data to understand where community need is highest and where silence signals danger.

Community partner

All data requests are reviewed personally by the founder. We are a small archive with a big mission — write to us and let's talk.
