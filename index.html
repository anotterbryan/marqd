About the Archive

Every dot is a story.
Every story is evidence.

Marq'd is a living archive of queer existence — geographically indexed, anonymously contributed, and built to last. People share firsthand stories tied to real-world coordinates, preserved as primary source history.

Primary sources are the most valuable form of historical evidence. For queer history, they have always been scarce — because they were dangerous to create. Letters burned. Diaries written in code. Meeting places demolished.

Marq'd is an act of preservation. Every story submitted joins a permanent, geographic record of queer life as it was actually lived.

How It Works

Anonymously. Permanently.

Drop a pin anywhere on the globe

Search for a place or use your coordinates. A city, a neighborhood, a specific corner. Anywhere something happened.

λ
Step 02
Tell us what happened

Write your story. Record your own voice or choose an AI reading. Add a photo of the place — never the people. No account required.

Your Marq is assigned a permanent ID — citable, searchable, part of the archive forever. The record exists now.
