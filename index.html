About the Archive
Every dot on this globe is a story.
Every story is evidence.

Marq'd is a living archive of queer existence — geographically indexed, anonymously contributed, and built to last. People share firsthand stories tied to real-world coordinates, preserving queer life as primary source history.

Primary sources are the most valuable form of historical evidence. For queer history, they have always been scarce — because they were dangerous to create. Letters burned. Diaries written in code. Meeting places demolished. Entire communities erased.

Marq'd is an act of preservation. Every story submitted — from São Paulo or Stockholm, from Cairo or Kansas City — joins a permanent, geographic record of queer life as it was actually lived.

Why This Matters
64
countries criminalize same-sex relationships.

In most of them, no official record of queer life exists. No archives. No history.
The record says: we were not there.

We were there. Marq'd is the proof.

How It Works
Contribute to the record.
Anonymously. Permanently.
λ
Step 01
Drop a pin anywhere on the globe

Use your location or search for a place. A city, a neighborhood, a specific intersection. Anywhere something happened.

λ
Step 02
Tell us what happened

Write your story. Choose a voice to have it read aloud. Add a photograph of the place — never the people. No account required.

λ
Step 03
Receive a permanent record number

Your Marq is assigned a Field Record ID. It is now part of the archive — permanent, citable, and yours.

Get Involved
One person.

Marq'd is in its founding stage — built by a single person who believes queer stories deserve to be preserved as the historical evidence they are. If you share that belief and have something to contribute, we want to hear from you.
