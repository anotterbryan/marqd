About the Archive

Every story is evidence.

Marq'd is a platform where queer people share anonymous firsthand stories tied to real-world coordinates. But it is more than a platform — it is a primary source archive of queer life across time and geography.

Primary sources are the most valuable form of historical evidence. For queer history, they have always been scarce — because they were dangerous to create and deadly to keep. Letters burned. Diaries written in code. Meeting places demolished. Oral histories lost with the people who held them.

Marq'd is an act of preservation. Stories submitted here are geographically anchored, timestamped, permanently numbered, and citable — exactly as a historian would cite any primary document.

64
countries criminalize same-sex relationships.
In most of them, no official record of queer life exists.
The record says: we were not there. We were there.
The Archive in Practice

What a Marq looks like.

Each story submitted becomes a Field Record — a permanent archive entry with a unique record number, a geographic coordinate, a timestamp, and the contributor's own words.

Stories can be accompanied by an AI-generated voice reading, or the contributor's own recorded voice — a form of oral history that preserves accent, emotion, and presence. Photographs of places and spaces (never people) add a documentary layer.

Every Field Record is citable. Researchers, historians, and journalists can reference a Marq'd entry the same way they would reference any primary source document. This is not accidental — it is the point.

How It Works

Leave your Marq.
Anonymously. Permanently.

λ
Step 01
Drop a pin anywhere on the globe

Use your location or search for a city, neighborhood, or intersection. Anywhere something happened.

λ
Step 02
Tell us what happened

Write your story. Choose a voice to read it aloud, or record your own. Add a photo of the place. No account required.

λ
Step 03
Receive a Field Record number

Your Marq is assigned a permanent ID — citable, searchable, and part of the archive forever.
