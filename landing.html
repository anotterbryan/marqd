About the Archive

Queer stories are primary sources.

In 1969, no one thought the notes slipped between strangers at the Stonewall Inn would become history. They were just moments — a touch, a look, a night. But those moments, accumulated and preserved, became the primary evidence of a community's existence, resistance, and humanity.

"In places where we cannot exist openly, we have always existed quietly. This is the record."

Marq'd makes it possible for anyone, anywhere, to contribute to that record — anonymously, safely, and permanently. Every story submitted adds to an irreplaceable geographic and temporal archive of queer existence.

Unlike a social media platform where content disappears or gets deprioritized, Marq'd treats its archive as permanent. Stories receive numbered, citable record IDs. They are evidence — not posts.

Why It Matters

64

countries criminalize same-sex relationships.

In most of them, no gay bars are listed on Google Maps. No Pride parades in the historical record. No archives of queer life. The official history says: we were not there.

12 countries impose the death penalty. In dozens more, LGBTQ+ people face state-sanctioned discrimination, forced conversion, and social erasure. Letters burned. Diaries written in code. Meeting places demolished. Oral histories lost.

Marq'd is the record that official history refuses to keep.

The Archive

How it works.

λ

Drop a pin anywhere on the globe

Any location. The intersection, the park, the street. The coordinates matter. Not just the city — the exact spot where something happened. Geographic specificity is historical evidence.

λ

Tell us what happened — text, voice, or both

You don't have to use names. You don't have to explain anything. What happened here? A spontaneous encounter in Madrid and a brave documentation from a country where that existence is illegal are both primary sources. Both matter.

λ

Your Marq is assigned a permanent record number

Every story receives a permanent, sequential record ID — format #MQ-004821. It is citable. It is a source. It counts. No government, institution, or majority vote can undo it.

Safety First

For contributors in hostile regions, anonymity is not a preference — it is a matter of physical safety. No personally identifiable information is ever collected from anonymous contributors. IPs are hashed, never stored in plaintext. The platform functions without an account.

The roadmap.

✓ Founding Site — Live

Establishing the Archive

The founding site establishes the project, communicates the mission, and invites early supporters and contributors before the full archive goes live.

  • Interactive 3D globe with seed data
  • Full mission and about pages
  • Contact and support infrastructure
  • Open Collective donation page
  • Public roadmap and architectural docs

⚙ Phase 1 — In Development

The Live Archive

A fully functional, live archive. Real stories. Real coordinates. Permanent records. A stranger anywhere in the world can read and submit — anonymously, safely, permanently — in under five minutes.

  • Live story submission (no account required)
  • Supabase backend — real-time globe
  • AI-read audio via ElevenLabs
  • Photo submission — places only
  • Academic citation export
  • Content moderation queue

◦ Phase 2 & 3 — Planned

Community & Institution

Optional identity and social connection without compromising the anonymous core. Then: Marq'd as a recognized resource for researchers, historians, journalists, and human rights advocates.

  • Optional user profiles
  • Time-scrubber — watch the archive grow
  • Legal context layer (ILGA World data)
  • Research export — JSON/CSV for scholars
  • Translations — 10+ languages
  • Tor-friendly access documentation
  • Institutional partnerships
